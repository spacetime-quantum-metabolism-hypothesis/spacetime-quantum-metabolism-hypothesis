## C21: Combination C1 + C14 -- Relativistic Quanta with Enhanced Cross-Section

**Label:** C21: Relativistic + 2-sigma (C1 x C14 combination)

**Equation:**

    dn_bar/dt + 4H*n_bar = Gamma_0 - 2*sigma*n_bar*rho_m

**Physical picture:**
Spacetime quanta are ultra-relativistic (w_sq=1/3), giving the 4H dilution coefficient from C1.
Simultaneously, the annihilation cross-section is doubled (as in C14, which gave the best chi2=11.28).
C1 pushes wa to ~-0.6 but worsens chi2; C14 improves chi2 but only reaches wa~-0.2.
Their combination may achieve both: the 4H coefficient suppresses quanta more in the past
(more negative wa) while the 2*sigma adjustment corrects the amplitude to maintain chi2 quality.

**Which B1 assumption this modifies:** H2 (w_sq=0 -> 1/3) AND H6 (sigma -> 2*sigma simultaneously)

**Dark energy prediction:**
Equilibrium: n_bar_eq = Gamma_0 / (2*sigma*rho_m + 4H).
At high z: suppression is faster than either C1 or C14 alone (both the H coefficient and sigma
are increased). Expect wa more negative than C1 (wa<-0.6). The amplitude relative to today is
controlled by normalization; chi2 may stay near C14 levels (~11.3).
Qualitative: w0 ~ -0.85, wa ~ -0.5 to -0.7.

**DESI improvement potential:** HIGH -- combines the two best individual modifications.

**Extra parameters:** 0 (both modifications are fixed choices, no free parameters)

## C22: Logarithmic Entropy Creation -- Bekenstein-Hawking Source

**Label:** C22: Entropy-Driven Creation (Bekenstein-Hawking)

**Equation:**

    dn_bar/dt + 3H*n_bar = Gamma_0 * ln(S_H / S_0) - sigma*n_bar*rho_m

where S_H = pi / (G*H^2) is the Hubble horizon entropy (in Planck units) and
S_0 = pi / (G*H_0^2) is its value today.

**Physical picture:**
The Bekenstein-Hawking entropy of the Hubble horizon S_H = A_H/(4*l_P^2) = pi/(G*H^2).
Holographic thermodynamics suggests spacetime quanta are created whenever the
horizon entropy increases. The creation rate is proportional to d(ln S_H)/dt ~ -2*dH/dt / H.
In the late universe where H is slowly varying, ln(S_H/S_0) ~ 2*ln(H_0/H(z)) which
grows modestly with z. This ties the creation of spacetime quanta directly to
the second law for gravitational horizons, requiring no new energy scale.

**Which B1 assumption this modifies:** H5 (Gamma_0 constant -> entropy-driven)

**Dark energy prediction:**
At high z: H(z) >> H_0, so S_H(z) << S_0, meaning ln(S_H/S_0) < 0.
The creation term DECREASES (or reverses sign) at high z -- fewer quanta created in
the past when the horizon was smaller. This gives stronger suppression of rho_DE at
high z: wa more negative than B1. At low z (H~H_0): ln factor ~ 0, creation
suppressed, n_bar approaches its equilibrium -- so w0 may be near -1.
Qualitative: w0 ~ -0.95, wa ~ -0.3 to -0.6.

**DESI improvement potential:** HIGH -- mechanism naturally suppresses early DE.

**Extra parameters:** 0 (S_H is fully determined by H(z), no free scale)

## C23: Holographic Screen Equation -- Padmanabhan-SQMH Hybrid

**Label:** C23: Holographic Screen (Padmanabhan hybrid)

**Equation:**

    dn_bar/dt + 3H*n_bar = Gamma_0 * (N_sur - N_bulk) / N_sur - sigma*n_bar*rho_m

where N_sur = A_H / (4*l_P^2) = pi/(G*H^2) is the number of surface degrees of freedom
and N_bulk = |W| / (0.5 * k_B * T_H) is the bulk equipartition count (W = total Komar energy).

**Physical picture:**
Padmanabhan's emergent gravity proposal: cosmic expansion drives toward
N_sur = N_bulk equilibrium. The SQMH creation rate is modulated by the
departure from holographic equilibrium: creation is enhanced when N_sur > N_bulk
(spacetime has "room" for more quanta) and suppressed when N_sur = N_bulk (equilibrium).
This naturally terminates the creation when the holographic bound is saturated,
providing a built-in cutoff absent in B1.

**Which B1 assumption this modifies:** H5 (Gamma_0 constant -> holographic-equilibration-driven)

**Dark energy prediction:**
At high z: N_bulk ~ rho_m * V_H / T_H grows faster than N_sur as the horizon was smaller.
The factor (N_sur - N_bulk)/N_sur could become negative at high z, providing a net sink
rather than source. This would sharply suppress rho_DE at early times.
Qualitative: w0 ~ -1.0, wa ~ -0.4 to -0.8. Possibly overshoots DESI.

**DESI improvement potential:** HIGH -- self-regulating mechanism with correct sign.

**Extra parameters:** 0 (both N_sur and N_bulk derived from H(z) and rho_m(z))

## C24: Casimir-Plate Analogy -- Boundary-Dependent Creation

**Label:** C24: Casimir Creation (horizon boundary)

**Equation:**

    dn_bar/dt + 3H*n_bar = Gamma_0 * (H_0 / H)^2 - sigma*n_bar*rho_m

**Physical picture:**
The Casimir effect produces a vacuum energy density proportional to 1/L^4
where L is the plate separation. Applied to cosmology, L ~ 1/H is the Hubble
radius (the "plate" separating observable from unobservable). The Casimir-like
vacuum creation rate scales as 1/L^4 ~ H^4, but normalized to H_0 gives H^2/H_0^2
for the density-like creation rate. At high z: H >> H_0, so (H_0/H)^2 << 1,
meaning FEWER quanta were created in the early universe. The creation is
strongest when the Hubble volume is largest (today), making dark energy genuinely
late-time dominant.

**Which B1 assumption this modifies:** H5 (Gamma_0 constant -> Casimir-like 1/H^2 suppression)

**Dark energy prediction:**
Equilibrium: n_bar_eq ~ Gamma_0*(H_0/H)^2 / (sigma*rho_m + 3H).
At z >> 1: both the (H_0/H)^2 factor and the 3H term suppress n_bar strongly.
Compared to B9 (which has H/H_0 in creation): this is the INVERSE -- creation is
suppressed at high z rather than enhanced. Much more negative wa than B9.
Qualitative: w0 ~ -1.0, wa ~ -0.5 to -1.0.

**DESI improvement potential:** HIGH -- creation suppression at high z is exactly the needed mechanism.

**Extra parameters:** 0

## C25: Running Sigma -- Asymptotic Safety Scale Dependence

**Label:** C25: Running Cross-Section (Asymptotic Safety)

**Equation:**

    dn_bar/dt + 3H*n_bar = Gamma_0 - sigma * (1 + nu * H^2/H_0^2) * n_bar * rho_m

where nu is a dimensionless running parameter (nu > 0 means stronger annihilation at high H).

**Physical picture:**
In Asymptotic Safety (AS) quantum gravity, the gravitational coupling G runs with
energy scale k as G(k) = G_0 / (1 + omega*k^2*l_P^2). For cosmological running
k ~ H, this gives G(H) ~ G_0*(1 - omega*H^2*t_P^2) at low H. Since sigma = 4*pi*G*t_P,
the running coupling gives sigma(H) ~ sigma_0*(1 + nu*H^2/H_0^2) where nu encodes
the AS running coefficient. At high z (large H): sigma is larger, quanta are more
efficiently annihilated, dark energy is suppressed. This is physically motivated by
the UV fixed point of quantum gravity.

**Which B1 assumption this modifies:** H6 (sigma constant -> H-dependent running coupling)

**Dark energy prediction:**
The enhanced sigma at high z depletes n_bar faster in the past.
For nu ~ 1: the correction is O(H^2/H_0^2) ~ O((1+z)^3) in matter domination.
This gives a power-law enhancement of annihilation at high z -> wa significantly more negative.
Qualitative: w0 ~ -0.9, wa ~ -0.3 to -0.8 depending on nu.

**DESI improvement potential:** HIGH -- directly controls wa via nu.

**Extra parameters:** 1 (nu: AS running coefficient, dimensionless, expected O(0.01-0.1) from AS literature)

## C26: Conformal Symmetry Restoration -- Scale-Invariant Limit

**Label:** C26: Conformal Limit (trace-free coupling)

**Equation:**

    dn_bar/dt + 4H*n_bar = Gamma_0 - sigma * n_bar * (rho_m - 3*p_m)

where rho_m - 3*p_m = T^mu_mu (trace of stress tensor) for matter.

**Physical picture:**
At high energies, gravity approaches conformal symmetry. The T^mu_mu coupling
(used to exclude radiation in B1) becomes the PRIMARY coupling when conformal invariance
is restored. For non-relativistic matter: T^mu_mu = rho_m - 3*(0) = rho_m (same as B1).
But the 4H dilution coefficient comes from the conformal weight of the quantum foam
density (conformal scalars dilute as a^{-4} like radiation). This is the conformally
symmetric version of SQMH: both the dilution AND the coupling respect conformal
covariance. The two modifications (4H from C1, T^mu_mu coupling) are not independent
when conformal symmetry is imposed -- they must appear together.

**Which B1 assumption this modifies:** H2 (w_sq -> 1/3 for conformal weight) AND H3/H7 
(coupling to T^mu_mu trace, coincidentally same as H7 for p_m=0 matter but structurally different)

**Dark energy prediction:**
For non-relativistic matter (p_m ~ 0): reduces to C1 (4H coefficient, same sigma*rho_m).
For relativistic matter (p_m = rho_m/3): coupling vanishes -- radiation does not annihilate
conformally coupled quanta. This is a STRONGER exclusion of radiation than C1.
Qualitative: same as C1 for z < z_eq, w0 ~ -0.9, wa ~ -0.4 to -0.6.
At z > z_eq: radiation dominates but coupling = 0, so quanta are only diluted by 4H.
The very different early-universe behavior could change CMB observables.

**DESI improvement potential:** MODERATE -- same late-time wa as C1 but cleaner theoretical basis.

**Extra parameters:** 0

## C27: Two-Component Foam -- Hot and Cold Quantum Populations

**Label:** C27: Bimodal Foam (two-temperature population)

**Equation:**

    dn_h/dt + 4H*n_h = Gamma_h - sigma*n_h*rho_m   (hot: w=1/3)
    dn_c/dt + 3H*n_c = Gamma_c - sigma*n_c*rho_m   (cold: w=0)
    n_bar = n_h + n_c,   Gamma_h + Gamma_c = Gamma_0

**Physical picture:**
Quantum gravity models (causal dynamical triangulations, spin foam) suggest spacetime
quanta are not monochromatic -- they have a spectrum of "temperatures" or energies.
The hot component (ultra-relativistic, w=1/3) is created at high-H epochs and dilutes
as a^{-4}. The cold component (w=0) is created uniformly and dilutes as a^{-3}.
At low z, the cold component dominates (less diluted); at high z, both are present.
The total dark energy rho_DE = mu*(n_h + n_c) has a composite evolution with
the hot component giving stronger z-dependence (more negative wa contribution).

**Which B1 assumption this modifies:** H2 (single w_sq -> bimodal distribution w=0 and w=1/3)

**Dark energy prediction:**
The dark energy equation of state is a weighted average of two components with
different dilution histories. At low z: cold dominates (w0 near -1). At high z:
hot component was relatively more abundant (less diluted from its 4H creation era).
Net effect: wa is between B1 (w=0 only) and C1 (w=1/3 only).
Qualitative: w0 ~ -0.93, wa ~ -0.2 to -0.4. Interpolates between B1 and C1.
Ratio Gamma_h/Gamma_c controls where on this interpolation.

**DESI improvement potential:** MODERATE -- controllable wa via hot/cold ratio.

**Extra parameters:** 1 (f_h = Gamma_h/Gamma_0: hot fraction, dimensionless, 0 to 1)

## C28: Gravitational Screening -- Yukawa-Modified Cross-Section

**Label:** C28: Yukawa Screening (finite range gravity)

**Equation:**

    dn_bar/dt + 3H*n_bar = Gamma_0 - sigma * exp(-m_g * c / (H * hbar)) * n_bar * rho_m

where m_g is a graviton mass scale (or equivalently, a screening mass for the
quantum-matter interaction), and the exponential gives Yukawa suppression.

**Physical picture:**
If the quantum-matter annihilation is mediated by a massive mediator (Yukawa-like
interaction with range lambda = hbar/(m_g*c)), the interaction is suppressed when the
Hubble radius 1/H drops below the Yukawa range. At high z: H is large, so c/H < lambda,
meaning the Hubble volume is smaller than the interaction range -- the interaction
operates at full strength. At low z: 1/H >> lambda, but the Yukawa factor is then ~1.
More precisely: the relevant scale is NOT 1/H but the inter-particle distance, which
scales as rho_m^{-1/3}. The screening exp(-m_g / (rho_m^{1/3})) suppresses annihilation
at low density (voids), reducing the effective sigma in underdense regions.
For the homogeneous background: the argument is m_g * t_P * (rho_crit/rho_m)^{1/3}.

**Rewritten for homogeneous background:**

    dn_bar/dt + 3H*n_bar = Gamma_0 - sigma * (1 - exp(-lambda_Y * rho_m^{1/3})) * n_bar * rho_m

where lambda_Y = m_Y / rho_P^{1/3} (dimensionless Yukawa screening parameter).

**Which B1 assumption this modifies:** H6 (sigma constant -> density-dependent Yukawa screening)

**Dark energy prediction:**
At high z (high rho_m): the Yukawa exponential -> 0, sigma_eff -> full sigma (more annihilation).
At low z (low rho_m): sigma_eff -> sigma * lambda_Y * rho_m^{1/3} (suppressed, less annihilation).
Net: MORE dark energy today (less annihilation at low density) compared to B1.
This shifts w0 to be less negative (closer to -1) and gives wa more negative
(dark energy was more suppressed in the past at full sigma).
Qualitative: w0 ~ -0.98, wa ~ -0.3 to -0.6.

**DESI improvement potential:** MODERATE -- correct direction for wa.

**Extra parameters:** 1 (lambda_Y: Yukawa screening scale, dimensionless)

## C29: Depletion Saturation -- Logistic Annihilation

**Label:** C29: Logistic Sink (saturation at high density)

**Equation:**

    dn_bar/dt + 3H*n_bar = Gamma_0 - sigma * n_bar * rho_m / (1 + rho_m / rho_sat)

where rho_sat is a saturation density (natural scale: rho_P * (t_P * H_0)^2 ~ rho_crit).

**Physical picture:**
In any physical annihilation process, the rate saturates when the matter density
exceeds the capacity of quantum foam to interact (analogous to enzyme kinetics:
Michaelis-Menten saturation). When rho_m >> rho_sat, the denominator grows as rho_m,
canceling the numerator: effective annihilation rate -> sigma * n_bar * rho_sat = const.
This is a natural upper bound on how fast quanta can be destroyed by matter.
At low density (rho_m << rho_sat): standard B1 mass-action. At high density: saturates.
The saturation means early-universe quanta were NOT efficiently annihilated even
at high matter density -- they "overflowed" the annihilation channel.

**Which B1 assumption this modifies:** H3 (linear mass-action -> saturating Michaelis-Menten form)

**Dark energy prediction:**
At high z (rho_m >> rho_sat): annihilation rate saturates, quanta accumulate more than B1.
This gives MORE dark energy in the past than B1 => wa LESS negative (wrong direction!).
At low z: identical to B1. So the saturation mechanism hurts DESI compatibility.
Qualitative: w0 ~ -0.9, wa ~ 0 to +0.1 (wrong direction for DESI).

**DESI improvement potential:** LOW -- saturation preserves early-universe quanta, wrong direction.

**Extra parameters:** 1 (rho_sat: saturation density, physical scale)

*Note: C29 is included as a physically well-motivated equation that nonetheless fails the
DESI direction criterion. It may be relevant for constraints in opposite direction.*

## C30: Carrollian Limit -- Ultra-Local Quantum Foam

**Label:** C30: Carrollian Foam (c -> 0 speed limit)

**Equation:**

    dn_bar/dt + 3H*n_bar = Gamma_0 - sigma * n_bar * rho_m * (1 + alpha_C * H^2 * t_P^2)

where alpha_C is a dimensionless Carrollian correction coefficient (expected O(1)).

**Physical picture:**
Carrollian symmetry is the c -> 0 limit of Lorentzian symmetry, relevant at the
Planck scale where the local light cone "collapses." In Carrollian gravity (de Boer et al.
2022, Donnay et al. 2019), quantum gravity near the big bang or horizon surfaces
approaches a Carrollian regime. In this limit, causality is ultra-local -- each spacetime
point evolves independently. The SQMH annihilation cross-section picks up a Carrollian
correction proportional to H^2*t_P^2 (the ratio of Hubble scale to Planck scale, squared).
This is small today (H_0^2*t_P^2 ~ 10^{-122}) but was O(1) near the Planck epoch.

**Which B1 assumption this modifies:** H6 (sigma constant -> H^2 Carrollian running)

**Dark energy prediction:**
Identical to C25 (running sigma) at the level of the background equation.
The Carrollian correction H^2*t_P^2 is so small at cosmological H that it is
completely negligible: alpha_C * H_0^2 * t_P^2 ~ 10^{-122} * alpha_C.
Qualitative: indistinguishable from B1 for any observational purpose.
Interesting theoretically (Planck-scale justified running) but numerically irrelevant.

**DESI improvement potential:** LOW -- Planck-suppressed correction, numerically identical to B1.

**Extra parameters:** 1 (alpha_C, but physically forced to make no observational difference)

*Note: C30 is the "theoretically honest version" of C25 -- if the running is truly derived from
Planck-scale physics (rather than phenomenological), it is too small to be seen.*

## C31: Information-Theoretic Annihilation -- Mutual Information Channel

**Label:** C31: Mutual Information Sink (Landauer-SQMH)

**Equation:**

    dn_bar/dt + 3H*n_bar = Gamma_0 - sigma * n_bar * rho_m * ln(rho_m / rho_ref)

where rho_ref is a reference density (natural choice: rho_crit,0).

**Physical picture:**
By Landauer's principle, erasing one bit of information costs k_B*T*ln(2) energy.
When a spacetime quantum is annihilated by matter, the information encoded in the
quantum's position/momentum must be transferred to matter. The annihilation rate is
proportional not just to the collision probability but also to the information content
of the matter, which scales as ln(rho_m/rho_ref) (Shannon entropy of density fluctuations).
At high density (rho_m > rho_ref): ln > 0, annihilation enhanced.
At low density (rho_m < rho_ref): ln < 0, the equation predicts CREATION from matter
when matter is underdense -- physically, underdense regions "emit" quanta.
This ties the creation/annihilation balance to the entropy of the matter distribution.

**Which B1 assumption this modifies:** H3 (linear mass-action -> logarithmic information-theory modification)

**Dark energy prediction:**
At high z (rho_m >> rho_ref): ln factor grows as ln((1+z)^3) ~ 3*ln(1+z).
Enhanced annihilation at high z => less dark energy in past => wa more negative.
At low z (rho_m ~ rho_ref): ln ~ 0, annihilation suppressed -- transition to DE dominated.
Qualitative: w0 ~ -0.95, wa ~ -0.3 to -0.5.
The log growth is slower than power-law, so improvement is moderate over B1.

**DESI improvement potential:** MODERATE -- logarithmic enhancement of annihilation at high z.

**Extra parameters:** 1 (rho_ref: reference density for information entropy, physical scale)
(If rho_ref = rho_crit,0 is fixed by normalization: 0 extra parameters)

## C32: Duality-Motivated -- T-duality Between Hubble and Planck Scales

**Label:** C32: T-duality Bounce (string-inspired)

**Equation:**

    dn_bar/dt + 3H*n_bar = Gamma_0 - sigma * n_bar * rho_m * (H_P^2 / H^2 + H^2 / H_P^2) / 2

where H_P = t_P^{-1} is the Planck Hubble rate.

**Physical picture:**
String theory T-duality maps the compactification radius R to 1/R (in string units).
For cosmology, this becomes a symmetry H <-> H_P^2/H. The duality-invariant cross-section
is the harmonic mean sigma_eff = sigma*(H_P/H + H/H_P)^2 / 4, or equivalently the
sum of the two dual contributions. For H << H_P (today): H_P^2/H^2 >> 1, so the
effective sigma is enhanced by (H_P/H)^2 ~ 10^{122}. This is enormous -- but since we
normalize to today's n_bar, the (H_P/H_0)^2 factor is absorbed into Gamma_0.
The RELATIVE change between z=0 and z>0 is what matters: sigma_eff(z)/sigma_eff(0) =
(H_0/H(z))^2 + (H(z)/H_0)^2 which grows as (1+z)^3 in matter domination.
This gives a strongly z-dependent effective sigma, dramatically suppressing early DE.

**Which B1 assumption this modifies:** H6 (sigma constant -> T-duality symmetric running)

**Dark energy prediction:**
The effective sigma scales as (H/H_0)^2 ~ (1+z)^3 at high z (dominant term).
This means the annihilation term grows as sigma*(1+z)^3 * n_bar * rho_m at high z,
vastly more efficient than B1's sigma*n_bar*rho_m.
The early-universe n_bar is therefore strongly suppressed: much less dark energy at high z.
Qualitative: w0 ~ -0.85, wa ~ -0.5 to -1.0 (potentially reaching DESI target).
The normalization today (H=H_0) gives sigma_eff = sigma*(1+1)/2 = sigma, so w0 unchanged.

**DESI improvement potential:** HIGH -- H^2 running of sigma gives strong z-dependence.

**Extra parameters:** 0 (H_P is the Planck rate, a fixed constant)

## C33: Resonance Annihilation -- Feshbach Resonance Analogy

**Label:** C33: Resonance Cross-Section (Feshbach-SQMH)

**Equation:**

    dn_bar/dt + 3H*n_bar = Gamma_0 - sigma * n_bar * rho_m * Gamma_res^2 / ((rho_m - rho_res)^2 + Gamma_res^2)

where rho_res is a resonance density and Gamma_res is the resonance width (both in kg/m^3).

**Physical picture:**
In atomic physics, Feshbach resonances cause the scattering cross-section to peak sharply
when the collision energy matches a bound state. By analogy, the quantum foam annihilation
cross-section may peak when the matter density matches the density of a "bound state" in the
quantum gravity vacuum (e.g., the Planck density rho_P, or the de Sitter vacuum density).
If rho_res ~ rho_crit,0, the annihilation is maximal today, making dark energy transition
from accumulating to being consumed. The Lorentzian profile is the simplest resonance shape.

**Which B1 assumption this modifies:** H3 (linear mass-action -> resonant cross-section)

**Dark energy prediction:**
If rho_res >> rho_m,0 (high-z resonance): enhanced annihilation at high z, wa more negative.
If rho_res ~ rho_m,0 (low-z resonance): maximal annihilation today, possible sharp transition.
The resonance profile is very model-dependent. Unless rho_res is derived from first principles,
this is essentially a 2-parameter phenomenological model. Not predictive without rho_res.
Qualitative: highly variable. Could give wa anywhere from +0.5 to -2 depending on rho_res.

**DESI improvement potential:** MODERATE (high if rho_res is at the right scale, but requires tuning)

**Extra parameters:** 2 (rho_res: resonance density; Gamma_res: resonance width) -- at the parameter limit

## C34: Wheeler-DeWitt Wavefunction -- Quantum Cosmology Creation Rate

**Label:** C34: WdW Creation Rate (quantum cosmology)

**Equation:**

    dn_bar/dt + 3H*n_bar = Gamma_0 * exp(-S_E(H) / hbar) - sigma*n_bar*rho_m

where S_E(H) = pi / (G*H^2) is the Euclidean de Sitter action (Hartle-Hawking tunneling action).

**Physical picture:**
In quantum cosmology, the probability of creating a de Sitter universe by tunneling
from nothing scales as exp(-S_E) = exp(-3*pi / (G*Lambda)) (Hartle-Hawking). Applied to
spacetime quantum creation: each quantum is a micro-de Sitter bubble, created by tunneling
with rate proportional to exp(-pi/(G*H^2)). At high H (early universe): S_E is small,
tunneling is fast, creation is enhanced. At low H (today): S_E ~ pi/(G*H_0^2) ~ 10^{122},
tunneling is utterly suppressed -- creation today is negligible, normalized only by Gamma_0.
Wait: at low H, exp(-S_E) ~ 0, which would mean NO creation today. This must be normalized:
the ratio exp(-S_E(H)) / exp(-S_E(H_0)) = exp(-(S_E(H) - S_E(H_0))).
Gamma_eff(z) = Gamma_0 * exp(pi/(G*H_0^2) - pi/(G*H(z)^2)) = Gamma_0 * exp(S_0 - S_E(H)).
For H > H_0: S_E < S_0, so Gamma_eff > Gamma_0 -- ENHANCED creation in the past.

**Which B1 assumption this modifies:** H5 (Gamma_0 constant -> WdW tunneling rate)

**Dark energy prediction:**
Enhanced creation at high z means MORE n_bar in the past, not less.
This gives wa > 0 (dark energy was HIGHER at high z than today) -- wrong direction for DESI.
Unless the annihilation term simultaneously dominates more strongly at high z, the net effect
is less negative wa than B1. Likely WORSE for DESI.
Qualitative: w0 ~ -0.9, wa > -0.1 (wrong direction).

**DESI improvement potential:** LOW -- enhanced early creation gives wrong wa sign.

**Extra parameters:** 0 (S_E is determined by H(z) and G, no new parameters)

## C35: Geometrostatic Coupling -- Cotton Tensor Source

**Label:** C35: Cotton Tensor Annihilation (3D conformal gravity)

**Equation:**

    dn_bar/dt + 3H*n_bar = Gamma_0 - sigma*n_bar*rho_m - beta_C * C_mnp * C^mnp * n_bar / H_0^2

where C_mnp is the Cotton tensor (3D conformal curvature) and beta_C is a coupling.
For homogeneous FLRW: C_mnp = 0 (FLRW is conformally flat), so this REDUCES to B1.
The Cotton tensor becomes non-zero in the perturbed universe (structure formation era).

**Physical picture:**
The Cotton tensor is the 3-dimensional analogue of the Weyl tensor, encoding
conformal curvature not captured by the Ricci tensor. In the perturbed universe,
massive structures (galaxies, clusters) generate non-zero Cotton curvature.
The SQMH annihilation rate in these environments picks up an extra term from
the local conformal curvature -- spacetime quanta are more efficiently destroyed
near conformally non-trivial structures (voids and filaments). This connects
the dark energy equation to large-scale structure formation.

**Which B1 assumption this modifies:** H1/H3 (FLRW and linear mass-action -> conformal curvature coupling)

**Dark energy prediction:**
At background (homogeneous) level: identical to B1. No change in w0 or wa.
At perturbation level: dark energy is inhomogeneous (varies with Cotton curvature).
This is a clustering dark energy model, not a background modification.

## C36: Viscous Quantum Foam -- Bulk Viscosity Correction

**Label:** C36: Viscous Foam (Israel-Stewart bulk viscosity)

**Physical picture:**
Real fluids have bulk viscosity that resists volume changes. A viscous quantum foam
would resist expansion: when the universe expands, the foam's viscous stress generates
an effective source term proportional to (theta)^2 = (3H)^2 where theta is the expansion
scalar. Israel-Stewart viscous hydrodynamics gives a relaxation equation for the bulk
pressure, but to leading order the bulk viscosity acts as an effective creation rate.
The viscous creation term 3*xi*H^2/(n_bar*m_q) grows at high z (high H), compensating
for depletion. This is the causal viscosity formulation (not Eckart acausal).

**Which B1 assumption this modifies:** H8 (no self-interaction -> viscous self-interaction)

**Dark energy prediction:**

This is similar to B9 (H-dependent creation) but with an n_bar-dependent denominator.
Near equilibrium: the viscous source balances at a different n_bar value.
This could give either more or less wa depending on the viscosity magnitude.
For xi_bulk > 0: viscous creation enhances n_bar at high z => wa less negative (wrong).
For xi_bulk < 0 (anti-viscous, second law violating): wa more negative (but unphysical).
Qualitative: xi_bulk > 0 gives w0 ~ -0.9, wa ~ 0 (wrong direction for DESI).

**DESI improvement potential:** LOW -- bulk viscosity creation counteracts depletion.

**Extra parameters:** 1 (xi_bulk: bulk viscosity, but only physical for xi_bulk > 0 which hurts DESI)

For sigma_d != sigma: interpolation between B1 and the 6x version.
Qualitative: w0 ~ -0.83, wa ~ -0.25 to -0.4.

**DESI improvement potential:** MODERATE -- similar mechanism to C14 but with dark matter motivation.

## C40: Apparent Horizon Area -- Trapping Horizon Creation Rate

**Label:** C40: Trapping Horizon Source (Hayward-SQMH)

**Equation:**

    dn_bar/dt + 3H*n_bar = Gamma_0 * (r_AH / r_AH,0)^2 - sigma*n_bar*rho_m

where r_AH = 1 / sqrt(H^2 + k/a^2) is the apparent (trapping) horizon radius
and r_AH,0 = 1/H_0 is its value today (for k=0 flat universe: r_AH = 1/H).

For flat FLRW (k=0):

    dn_bar/dt + 3H*n_bar = Gamma_0 * (H_0/H)^2 - sigma*n_bar*rho_m

**Physical picture:**
The apparent (trapping) horizon in cosmology is the surface where outgoing null rays are
momentarily trapped -- it is the cosmological analogue of the black hole horizon.
Hayward (1998) showed this is the physically meaningful horizon for thermodynamics.
Spacetime quanta are created by the Hawking-like radiation of this surface: creation rate
proportional to the area A_AH = 4*pi*r_AH^2 ~ 1/H^2. Larger horizon (smaller H, today)
= more surface area = more creation. Smaller horizon (larger H, past) = less creation.
This is the Casimir mechanism (C24) but derived from the specific geometric object
(apparent horizon) rather than a Casimir plate analogy.

**Which B1 assumption this modifies:** H5 (Gamma_0 constant -> apparent horizon area-driven)

**Dark energy prediction:**
This is mathematically identical to C24: Gamma_eff = Gamma_0*(H_0/H)^2.
At high z: H >> H_0, so (H_0/H)^2 << 1, creation nearly zero. Dark energy builds
from low z only. Strong suppression of early dark energy: wa very negative.
Equilibrium: n_bar_eq ~ Gamma_0*(H_0/H)^2 / (sigma*rho_m + 3H).
At z~1: H ~ 1.7*H_0, factor = 1/2.9 -- 65% suppression already.
At z~2: H ~ 2.8*H_0, factor = 1/7.7 -- 87% suppression.
The rapidly growing suppression gives a strongly evolving dark energy.
Qualitative: w0 ~ -0.97, wa ~ -0.6 to -1.0 (potentially reaching DESI target).

**DESI improvement potential:** HIGH -- geometric motivation, zero parameters, strong wa.

**Extra parameters:** 0 (r_AH is fully determined by H(z), no new physics scale)
