# 1. Introduction

## 1.1 The DESI DR2 tension

The DESI DR2 BAO release (Adame et al., arXiv:2503.14738) confirmed the
DR1 hint that a time-varying dark-energy equation of state is preferred
over ΛCDM at the ~2.8σ level, with joint DESI+CMB+DESY5-SN giving
w₀ = −0.757 ± 0.058, w_a = −0.83 ± 0.24.  The preference for **w_a < 0**
— energy flowing *into* matter and radiation from the dark sector as the
universe expands — is the key structural signal that any viable alternative
must reproduce.

Two further constraints sharpen the problem:

1. **No phantom crossing.** A w(z) that crosses −1 is generically
   associated with ghost modes or non-unitary scalar sectors.  Both
   thawing quintessence and standard quintessence families that attempt
   to reproduce the DESI signal almost always require a phantom
   excursion, which we take as a hard exclusion criterion.
2. **Cassini |γ−1| < 2.3 × 10⁻⁵.** Any modification of gravity that
   survives the DESI cosmological signal must also pass the Cassini
   tracking-range bound on the post-Newtonian parameter γ.  This rules
   out essentially all naive scalar-tensor and coupled-quintessence
   models that lack a Vainshtein or screening mechanism.

## 1.2 The Spacetime Quantum Metabolism Hypothesis

We propose that the tension between these three constraints is
structurally resolved if dark energy is **not** a new field or a new
fluid, but rather the net generation rate of a discrete spacetime
microstructure: a *metabolism*.  The SQMH axioms are stated in §2; here
we summarise the main physical consequence.

If matter annihilates discrete spacetime quanta with a universal
cross-section σ = 4πG·t_P (fixed by Newtonian matching) and the vacuum
generates quanta at a universal rate Γ₀ (fixed by the present balance),
then the effective dark-energy density obeys a modified continuity
equation sourced by the matter density.  In the homogeneous cosmological
limit, this produces a background w(z) that drifts from w ≈ −1 in the
past to a slightly less negative value today — i.e. w_a < 0 — with
**no** phantom crossing, and with the scalar mode of the metric
projected out in the static spherical PPN limit (so γ−1 = 0 exactly).

The L0 and L1 invariants (σ, Γ₀) carry no free parameters once
(G, t_P, H₀, Ω_m, Ω_Λ) are fixed.  The hypothesis therefore makes
falsifiable zero-parameter predictions; the goal of this paper is to
determine which effective field realisations of SQMH actually pass all
relevant joint constraints.
