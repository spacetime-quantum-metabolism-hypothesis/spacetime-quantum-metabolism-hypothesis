# Section 1: Introduction

## 1.1 The unsolved cosmological puzzles

The standard cosmological model, ΛCDM, despite its remarkable empirical
success, leaves several fundamental questions unanswered. The cosmological
constant Λ remains an unexplained dimensional input whose observed value
of approximately 10^-122 in Planck units conflicts with naive quantum
field theory estimates by ~120 orders of magnitude [Weinberg 1989,
Carroll 2001]. Galactic rotation curves consistently exhibit the
empirical regularity of Milgrom's modified Newtonian dynamics (MOND),
where deviation from Newton occurs below an acceleration scale a_0 ≈
1.20 × 10^-10 m/s^2 [Milgrom 1983, McGaugh 2016]. The remarkable
coincidence a_0 ≈ c·H_0/(2π) [Famaey & McGaugh 2012] suggests that
this scale may be cosmological in origin, yet no microscopic mechanism
explains why a_0 takes this specific value.

Recent observations have introduced additional tensions. The DESI
collaboration's BAO measurements suggest a time-varying dark energy
equation-of-state w_a < 0 at ~3-4σ [DESI 2024]. The S_8 = σ_8·sqrt(Ω_m/0.3)
parameter measured from cosmic shear (DES Y3 [DES 2022]) and CMB
extrapolation (Planck 2018) shows ~3σ tension. The Hubble constant
H_0 inferred from CMB (~67.4 km/s/Mpc) and from local Cepheid+SN Ia
measurements (~73.0 km/s/Mpc) disagree at ~5σ [Riess+ 2022]. JWST
observations of massive galaxies at z > 8 challenge the standard
structure formation timeline [Labbe+ 2023].

Modified gravity approaches have attempted to address subsets of these
issues. MOND [Milgrom 1983] explains galactic rotation curves but
fails at cluster scales [Sanders 2003]. AQUAL [Bekenstein-Milgrom 1984]
provides a relativistic extension but doesn't address dark energy.
Verlinde's entropic gravity [Verlinde 2017] derives MOND-like behavior
from holographic principles but lacks specific quantitative predictions.
Running vacuum models [Sola+ 2024] provide phenomenological time
evolution of Λ but don't connect to galactic dynamics.

## 1.2 Spacetime Quantum Theory

We present Spacetime Quantum Theory (SQT), a unified framework where
matter consumes spacetime quanta (giving rise to gravity) while empty
space generates them (driving cosmic acceleration). The theory rests
on six axioms governing the interaction between a scalar quantum
density field n(x,t) and matter density ρ_m(x,t). With five free
parameters in the Branch B regime structure (Section 3), SQT
simultaneously addresses:

1. **Cosmological constant origin**: Λ_eff = (n_∞·ε)/c^2 where
   ε ~ ℏH_0 is the per-quantum energy and n_∞ is the steady-state
   cosmic quantum density.

2. **Milgrom's a_0 derivation**: The relationship a_0 = c·H_0/(2π)
   emerges from the coupling σ_0·ρ_crit·c combined with disc
   azimuthal projection 1/(2π).

3. **MOND cluster failure**: The Branch B regime structure
   (cosmic/cluster/galactic) explains why MOND succeeds galactically
   but fails at cluster scales.

4. **S_8 tension**: The reduced cluster regime coupling σ_cluster <
   σ_galactic naturally accounts for the observed cosmic shear
   discrepancy with CMB extrapolation.

5. **GR limit**: In the σ_0 → 0 limit, SQT reduces to ΛCDM with
   Λ derived from the quantum sector.

## 1.3 Predictions and tests

SQT makes 14 unique falsifiable predictions distinguishable from
ΛCDM, MOND, AQUAL, and Verlinde gravity. Eleven existing-data tests
have been verified: Big Bang nucleosynthesis abundances [L83],
GW170817 dispersion bound |c_g - c|/c < 10^-15 [L87], cosmic
chronometer H(z) data [L88], post-Newtonian parameter γ = 1
(Cassini limit) [L89], Mercury perihelion advance, lunar laser
ranging G_dot/G < 10^-13/yr, EHT shadow of M87* and Sgr A* [L99],
CMB acoustic peak structure [L106], pulsar timing array stochastic
GW background [L107], H0LiCOW lens time delay [L109], and BTFR
slope = 4 [L119].

Decisive tests await DESI DR3 (2027), MICROSCOPE-2 (2027), SKA
Phase 1 (2028-2030), ATLAS-3D (2025-2030), DES Y6 (2025), and CMB-S4
(2030). Each test addresses specific SQT predictions with current
or near-term experimental sensitivity.
