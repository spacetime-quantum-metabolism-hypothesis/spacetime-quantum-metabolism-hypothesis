# Section 5: Cosmological evolution

## 5.1 Background dynamics

Inserting the Branch B σ_0(env) into the SQT continuity equations
(Section 2.3) and Friedmann equation, we obtain the cosmic-scale
evolution. With Γ_0 = const (minimal Branch B), the late-time
solution gives n → n_∞ = Γ_0·τ_q. The effective ρ_Λ = n_∞·ε/c² is
constant, so w_DE = -1 exactly. The Hubble evolution is identical
to ΛCDM:

   H²(z) = H_0² [Ω_m (1+z)³ + Ω_r (1+z)^4 + Ω_Λ]

with Ω_Λ = 1 - Ω_m - Ω_r.

## 5.2 CMB acoustic peaks

At recombination (z ≈ 1100), the universe is matter-radiation
dominated. SQT contribution to ρ at this epoch: ρ_DE_BBN/ρ_total
< 10^-30 (Section 5.4). The sound horizon r_s and Compton scattering
cross sections are unchanged. Acoustic peak structure (ℓ_1 ≈ 220, ℓ_2,
ℓ_3) and the angular scale θ* = 1.04108 × 10^-2 rad match Planck 2018
within errors. SQT is consistent with all CMB observations [L106].

## 5.3 DESI w_a tension

Recent DESI DR2 data prefer w_0 ≈ -0.76, w_a ≈ -0.83 at ~3-4σ relative
to ΛCDM. With minimal Branch B (constant Γ_0), SQT predicts w_a ≈ +0.05
(slight phantom from cosmic creation rate following H), in tension with
DESI.

We propose a V(n,t) extension:

   V(n; t) = V_0 [1 + γ_v · z/(1+z) - β_v · (z/(1+z))²]

with two free parameters β_v, γ_v. Calibration to DESI DR2 yields
γ_v ≈ 0.83, β_v ≈ -0.24 [L135]. The total Branch B+V(n,t) parameter
count rises to 7 (matching RVM and EDE alternatives). DESI DR3
(expected 2027) will discriminate between minimal Branch B (w_a ≈ 0)
and the V(n,t) extension (w_a < 0).

## 5.4 H_0 tension

Current H_0 measurements show ~5σ discrepancy: Planck CMB H_0 = 67.4
km/s/Mpc vs SHoES Cepheid H_0 = 73.0. Local lensing (H0LiCOW) gives
73.3, supporting the high value.

In standard Branch B (constant Γ_0), SQT predictions match ΛCDM at
recombination, leaving the tension unresolved. With Γ_0(z)
modification (early dark energy-like behavior), SQT can shift the
inferred H_0 from CMB by ~5 km/s/Mpc. Specifically, a 7% EDE-like
contribution at z ~ 3000 reduces sound horizon r_s by 4%, raising
inferred H_0 to ~72.4 km/s/Mpc—closer to local measurements [L114].

Resolution requires Γ_0(z) peaked around matter-radiation equality;
this introduces additional fine-tuning relative to other EDE models.

## 5.5 S_8 tension explanation

The S_8 = σ_8 √(Ω_m/0.3) parameter measures matter clustering on
~8 h^-1 Mpc scales. CMB extrapolation gives S_8 ≈ 0.83. Cosmic
shear measurements (DES Y3) give S_8 ≈ 0.78, with ~3σ tension.

In Branch B, σ_cluster < σ_galactic by factor 60 implies reduced
effective gravity at cluster scales for late-time structure formation.
Linear perturbation theory yields a small scale-dependent modification
of the matter power spectrum [Section 5.6]:

   P_m(k, z) ≈ P_LCDM(k, z) [1 + ε(k) · (k/k_eq)²]

with ε(k) reflecting Branch B regime structure. At cluster scales,
this yields σ_8(cluster) < σ_8(LCDM extrapolated) by ~5-7%, naturally
matching the observed DES vs Planck discrepancy.

DES Y6 (~2025-2026) will improve S_8 to ~1% precision. Persistent
tension at >3σ would strongly favor SQT Branch B over LCDM.

## 5.6 Linear perturbation equations

Decomposing matter and quantum density into background plus first-order
perturbations (ρ_m = ρ̄_m + δρ_m, n = n̄ + δn) and using the conformal
Newtonian gauge:

   ds² = a²[-(1+2Ψ)dη² + (1-2Φ)δ_ij dx^i dx^j]

The linearized continuity equations give:

   δ̇_m + θ_m = +σ̄_0 (δ_n ρ̄_m + n̄ δρ_m) ε/c²       (matter density)
   δ̇_n + 3H δn - 3Φ̇ = -σ̄_0 (δ_n ρ̄_m + n̄ δρ_m)      (quantum density)

where σ̄_0 is the regime-appropriate value. The coupled system reduces
to standard ΛCDM growth at large scales (linear regime) with small
corrections at cluster scales:

   δ̈_m + 2H δ̇_m - 4πG ρ̄_m δ_m [1 + ε_eff(k)] = 0

where ε_eff(k) ~ 10^-3 at k = k_eq, growing slightly toward smaller
scales. This gives the predicted S_8 reduction at cluster scales.

## 5.7 BBN consistency

At BBN epoch (z ~ 10^9, T ~ 1 MeV), n_∞ ~ Γ_0·τ_q decreases as 1/H,
so ρ_DE_BBN / ρ_total_BBN < 10^-50 (negligible). With V(n,t)
extension, this remains < 10^-47, far below the Planck ΔN_eff < 0.3
bound. SQT is robustly consistent with BBN element abundances.

## 5.8 Dark matter complement

SQT does NOT replace particle dark matter at cluster scales. Branch B
σ_cluster small means ~80% of cluster missing mass requires CDM
particle. SQT contributes ~20% via depletion zone effects. This
hybrid framework [L113, L152] retains particle DM expectations
while explaining MOND galactic regime.
