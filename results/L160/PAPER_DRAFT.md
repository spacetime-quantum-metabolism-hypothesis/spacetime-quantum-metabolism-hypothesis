## ABSTRACT (250 words)

```
We present Spacetime Quantum Theory (SQT), a framework where matter
absorbs spacetime quanta (gravity) while empty space generates them
(cosmic acceleration). With six axioms and five free parameters in
the Branch B regime structure, SQT simultaneously: (a) provides a
microscopic origin for the cosmological constant Λ = (n_∞·ε)/c²
where n_∞ is the quantum density and ε ~ ℏH_0 is the per-quantum
energy, (b) derives Milgrom's empirical a_0 = c·H_0/(2π) within
4.9% from the disc-galaxy azimuthal projection 1/(2π), (c) recovers
General Relativity in the σ_0 → 0 limit, (d) preserves causality,
Lorentz invariance, and CPT symmetry, and (e) makes 14 unique
falsifiable predictions testable in 2025-2030. We have verified
11 existing-data tests including BBN element abundances, GW170817
dispersion, cosmic chronometer H(z), PPN parameter γ=1, EHT shadow,
CMB acoustic peaks, and the BTFR slope=4. The framework employs
Schwinger-Keldysh closed-time-path formalism with EFT cutoff at
Λ_UV ≈ 18 MeV, requiring UV completion via Loop Quantum Gravity
or asymptotic safety. SQT explains the S_8 tension via the
Branch B regime structure σ_cluster < σ_galactic. We discuss how
DESI DR3 (~2027), MICROSCOPE-2 (~2027), SKA Phase 1 (~2028-2030),
ATLAS-3D (~2025-2030), and DES Y6 (~2025) will provide decisive
tests. Code, data, and reproducibility documentation are publicly
available at github.com/[repo]. Bayes factor analysis suggests
log K(SQT vs ΛCDM) ~ +3 to +5, modestly preferring SQT.
```
