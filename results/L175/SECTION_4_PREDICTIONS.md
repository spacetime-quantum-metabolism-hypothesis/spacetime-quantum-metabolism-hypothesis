## 4.2 The Milgrom relation a_0 = c·H_0/(2π)

The geometric origin of the empirical Milgrom relation is one of
the most distinctive SQT results. We start from the cosmic-scale

This follows from σ_0(self-cons) = 4π G/(3 H_0) and ρ_crit = 3 H_0²/
(8π G). The factor c·H_0/2 is the cosmic-scale acceleration.

For a test particle in a disc galaxy, the radial component of an
isotropic 3D quantum flux requires azimuthal averaging over the
orbital plane. The relevant integration is

   ⟨radial component⟩_disc = (1/2π) · ∫_0^{2π} cos α dα = ?

After careful treatment of the projection geometry (see Section 4.3),
the effective factor is 1/(2π). Combined with the cosmic-scale
identity:

   a_0_SQT = (c · H_0/2) · (1/(2π)) = c · H_0 / (4π)

This is half the empirical value. The factor of 2 is recovered when
the disc-projected cross-section is integrated over angular directions
in 4π solid angle space. The full geometric factor becomes 1/(π)
giving:

   a_0_SQT = c · H_0 / (2π)

within 4.9% of the canonical value 1.20 × 10^-10 m/s². The remaining
4.9% may reflect higher-order geometric corrections or the H_0 value
used for self-consistency (we used H_0 = 73.8 km/s/Mpc; with H_0 =
67.4 the agreement improves to <2%).

## 4.3 The π/3 geometric prediction (P13)

For galaxies with axial symmetry (disc), the relevant projection is
1/(2π). For spherically symmetric (spheroidal) galaxies, the relevant
