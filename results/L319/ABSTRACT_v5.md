We present Spacetime Quantum Metabolism Theory (SQT), a phenomenological framework in
which spacetime carries a quantum metabolic load whose creation/annihilation balance
sources the observed cosmological constant. The theory rests on six independent axioms
plus five derived consequences. Within Branch B (anchor-driven), the metabolic cross

with ρ_q/ρ_Λ = 1.0000 under the E(0)=1 background normalisation, providing a candidate
phenomenological origin for Λ that is consistent with current background data without
introducing a tuned dark-energy potential. We confront SQT with DESI BAO (DR1+DR2),
Pantheon+ and DES-Y5 supernovae, compressed Planck CMB, and RSD f σ_8 measurements.
The model fits joint background data at LCDM-comparable χ², and reproduces the DESI
DR2 preference for w_a<0 through a Branch-B-specific late-time mechanism rather than
through a phantom-crossing scalar. We are explicit about limitations: SQT is a
background-level theory, and within the GW170817-locked, μ_eff≈1 sector it does not
relieve the σ_8 or H_0 tensions; any apparent improvement traces to Ω_m re-allocation
and is reported as such. Seven falsifiers — including DESI DR3 BAO w(z), CMB-S4 θ*,
LSST/Euclid 3x2pt growth, GW170817-class standard sirens, and ACT/SPT high-ℓ residuals
— will discriminate SQT against ΛCDM at 2–3σ within the next 24 months.

## Cover Letter (JCAP submission)

Dear Editor,

We submit for your consideration the manuscript "Spacetime Quantum Metabolism Theory:
A Phenomenological, Falsifiable Framework for the Cosmological Constant and Late-Time
Dynamics" for publication in the Journal of Cosmology and Astroparticle Physics.

The manuscript proposes SQT, a background-level cosmological framework built on six
independent axioms with five derived consequences. The theory delivers a candidate
phenomenological origin for the cosmological constant (ρ_q/ρ_Λ = 1.0000 under the
standard E(0)=1 normalisation), reproduces the DESI DR2 preference for w_a<0 through
its Branch-B (anchor-driven) three-regime structure, and yields 22 quantitative
predictions of which seven are testable with near-term experiments.

We have made a deliberate effort to present the framework honestly:

1. **Structural limitations are stated up front.** SQT is background-level. Within the
   GW170817-locked, μ_eff ≈ 1 sector it does not relieve the σ_8 or H_0 tensions, and
   we do not claim that it does. Any apparent S_8 movement in our analysis traces to
   Ω_m re-allocation and is reported as such.
2. **Phenomenological versus dynamical.** The ρ_q/ρ_Λ = 1.0000 match is a
   phenomenological identity under E(0)=1 normalisation, not a first-principles
   derivation, and is labelled accordingly throughout.
3. **Independence from related proposals.** We do not claim SQT is identical to, nor
   subsumes, the Maggiore–Mancarella nonlocal-gravity programme; phenomenological
   parallels at the background level are noted but not conflated.
4. **Evidence statistics.** We separately report fixed-θ and fully marginalised
   Bayesian evidences, with explicit Occam corrections.

The framework is **falsifiable on a near-term timeline**. Within roughly 24 months,
seven independent observations — DESI DR3 BAO w(z), CMB-S4 θ* shift, LSST-Y1 3x2pt
growth, Euclid spectroscopic f σ_8, GW170817-class standard sirens, ACT/SPT high-ℓ
residuals, and Stage-IV weak-lensing S_8 — will each individually discriminate SQT
from ΛCDM at the 2–3σ level. DESI DR3 alone activates three of these channels, and we
view its imminent release as a particularly timely test.

For potential editorial handling, we respectfully suggest cosmology editors with
expertise in dark-energy phenomenology and BAO analysis (e.g. an editor of Prof. Kim's
profile, JCAP). We have no conflicts of interest to declare and the manuscript is
not under consideration elsewhere.

We thank the editor and referees in advance for their consideration.

Sincerely,
