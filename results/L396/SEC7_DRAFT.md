# Section 7 — Outlook

This section sketches what the next observational cycle, the next decade of
facilities, and a possible companion paper can decide about the phenomenology
reported in Sections 3–6. Nothing here changes the headline numbers; everything
is forward-looking.

## 7.1 DR3 timeline

The discriminating power of present-day BAO data between the SQT champion
family identified in Sections 4–5 and the leading mainstream alternates
(Maggiore–Mancarella RR non-local gravity; f(Q) with n ≥ 1; the 14-cluster
canonical drift class represented by A12) is limited. A Fisher pairwise forecast
on DR2-equivalent volumes shows the two mainstream families separated by only
about 0.2 sigma; SQT vs LCDM is a few times larger but still well under the
threshold at which BAO alone would settle the question.

DESI DR3 raises the effective volume by roughly a factor 1.8 over DR2. Under
that scaling the mainstream pair stays sub-sigma, while the SQT champion
breaks degeneracy primarily through the *shape* of the low-redshift transition
in the dark-sector contribution rather than through the (w0, wa) projection.
Concretely, DR3 will fix five quantities that the analysis of Sections 4–5 is
already framed around: the BGS volume-averaged distance at z = 0.295; the
LRG transverse and radial distances at z = 0.51, 0.706, 0.93; the ELG point
at z = 1.32; the QSO point at z = 1.49; and the Lyman-alpha point at z = 2.33.
For each of these the SQT champion predicts a definite sign and approximate
magnitude of departure from the LCDM-Planck baseline — the predictions are
fixed by the parameters reported in Section 5 and are not retuned here.

Two operational rules govern the DR3 phase. First, the analysis pipeline at
simulations/l6/dr3/ must remain idle until the DR3 release becomes public; the
forecasts above use only the published DR2 covariance and the announced DR3
sample sizes. Second, at the moment of release the joint (BAO + SN + CMB +
RSD) chain replaces, rather than supplements, the BAO-only conclusions of
Section 4 — in particular, BAO-only low-Ω_m optima are not cosmological
statements and will not be quoted as such after DR3.

## 7.2 Facility map

The model class studied here is falsifiable along several independent
channels. The next-decade facility map below distributes those channels across
the facilities that will tighten them, and states honestly what SQT predicts
in each case. SQT does not need a dedicated mission — its survival or failure
is decided as a by-product of programs already funded.

| Channel | Facility (≤ 2030) | SQT prediction / role |
|---|---|---|
| Background w(z), low-z transition | DESI DR3, Euclid Y1 spectroscopic | Direct test of the transition shape in the SQT champion; this is the strongest discriminator |
| Growth f sigma_8 | DESI RSD DR2/DR3, Euclid 3×2pt, Subaru PFS | The SQT background-only solution gives G_eff/G ≈ 1, so f sigma_8 is predicted to track LCDM within ~1 % — null-test target |
| S_8 / weak lensing | LSST Y1, Euclid WL, Roman | The SQT champion does **not** relieve S_8 tension; this is structural, since mu_eff ≈ 1 throughout. The honest prediction is "no S_8 signal" |
| GW standard sirens | LIGO O5, ET / CE pathfinder | Independent H0 anchor; tests c_T = c branches and constrains any disformal extension with A′ ≠ 0 |
| Compressed CMB | SPT-3G, ACT legacy, CMB-S4 (~2030+) | Tightens the theta_star floor below the Hu–Sugiyama 0.3 % accuracy that currently dominates the compressed CMB error budget; sharpens (omega_m, h) joint posterior |
| 21 cm intensity mapping | CHIME ongoing, HIRAX, SKA-Mid | Cross-checks BAO at z ~ 1–3 with orthogonal systematics — failure mode for any spectroscopic-only artifact |

The only row where SQT makes a non-null prediction that mainstream alternates
do not share is the first one: the *shape* of the low-redshift transition in
g(z). The other rows are diagnostics that protect against the model being
right for the wrong reasons.

## 7.3 Companion paper

The main paper retains the SQMH-derived framing because the amplitude-locking
result of Section 5 is partially anchored in the underlying construction
(metabolic source term tied to Ω_m), not purely fitted. Two questions of the
internal Q-list — Q13 (S_8 channel) and Q14 (independent growth channel) —
are not jointly satisfied by current data, so the headline target remains
JCAP rather than a PRD Letter.

A phenomenology-only companion paper is prepared as a defensive split. Its
scope is the L33 functional-form scan, the grid champions of L46–L48, and the
AICc / BIC / Bayesian-evidence comparison tables of L5 and L6, presented
without the SQMH derivation chapter. The companion is not submitted by
default; it is released either if a referee asks for the theory–data
separation explicitly, or if DR3 confirms the L33 family at Δχ² > 4 against
LCDM in the joint chain. In either case the companion will not claim that
"data prefer the extra parameter": the marginalized Bayesian evidence gap
remains within the Occam penalty for the current dataset, and that statement
is recorded as a hard constraint on the companion's wording.

The two-paper design also clarifies attribution: the C28 (Maggiore–Mancarella
RR non-local) comparator that appears in Sections 4 and 5 is an independent
mainstream theory and is treated as such throughout — both papers cite it as
a comparator rather than as part of the SQT family, in line with the
project's standing rule.
