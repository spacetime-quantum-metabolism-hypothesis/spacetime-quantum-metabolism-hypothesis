## Conclusion

- **f_1 > 0 branch**: well-posed at all z in the scan, CPL extraction
  gives `w_a < 0` monotonically.  For n = 1.5, w_a decreases smoothly
  from 0 at f_1 = 0 down to -2.13 at f_1 = +0.30.  For n = 2, 3 the
  decrease is stronger and saturates at the fit bound (-3) for
  |f_1| >= 0.05.
- **f_1 < 0 branch**: the Friedmann equation (**) has two real roots
  for small |f_1|; brentq selects the LCDM-continuation root which is
  well-defined.  CPL extraction on this branch yields `w_a > 0` (for
  n = 1.5, 2.0) and is therefore **excluded** by DESI (w_a < 0).
  For n >= 3 and |f_1| >= 0.08 the low-E root collapses and the fit
  lands at the CPL bound (-3), i.e. the branch loses physical
  interpretation at large |f_1|.
- **L2 R3 verification** (f_1 > 0 -> w_a < 0) is confirmed by the
  full Frusciante Friedmann equation at L4.
- **L3 low-z expansion toy** used
  `rho_de = OL0 [1 + f_1 (a^alpha - 1)]`, a sign-different
  parameterisation that inverted the role of f_1.  That is the
  source of the L3 sign discrepancy; it is a toy artefact, **not a
  new branch of the theory**.

## Final sign verification result

**Physical, DESI-consistent branch: f_1 > 0 (w_a < 0).**
L2 R3 correct; L3 toy sign inversion is a parameterisation artefact.
